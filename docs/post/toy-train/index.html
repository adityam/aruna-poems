Heritage Toy Train

The Kalka-Shima railway line prvided a comfortable means of transport from the plains to the mountanous regions of Shima, which was the summer capital of the British Raj. Built between 1898 and 1903, this 96.6km single narrow-guage line passes through 102 tunnels, 864 bridges, and 918 curves and was an engineering feat. In fact, even now, the multi-arched viaduct gallery bridge at Kanoh is the highest in India. In 2008, UNESCO gave the status of Heritage Toy Train to his railway line.
